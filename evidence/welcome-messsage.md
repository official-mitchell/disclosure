SUPPORTING INTEL

**Q: What is “Disclosure"?**
A: Disclosure is not a press conference and it’s not a single revelation. Disclosure is the process by which governments, institutions, and the public are forced to confront that something non-human, non-conventional, or fundamentally unknown is interacting with our world, and that this has been managed, hidden, or misunderstood for decades. Think of it less like ripping off a bandage and more like discovering the house was built on a fault line.

**Q: UAP vs UFO**
A: UFO means Unidentified Flying Object, which assumes flight and assumes an object. UAP means Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena, which intentionally removes assumptions. UAP can involve air, sea, space, sensors, cognition, or systems behaving in ways that break known models. The language changed because the problem got bigger than “things in the sky.”

**Q: Are “aliens” the same as Disclosure?**
A: Not necessarily. “Aliens” are one possible explanation. Disclosure is about acknowledging that current explanations are incomplete, that observed behaviors violate known limits, and that human institutions may not be the dominant actors they assumed they were. The unsettling part is not what it is, but what it implies about control, deterrence, and truth.

TAKEAWAYS

1. You do not need perfect information to act, but acting without understanding who you endanger is how this goes wrong.

2. Trust is a resource. Once spent, it does not regenerate. Choose carefully who you share information with and why.

3. Winning the game does not mean being right. It means surviving the consequences of being right.
